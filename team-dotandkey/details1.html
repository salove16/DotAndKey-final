Melatonin for Peaceful Sleep: Does it really work?


Is melatonin the answer to your sleep problems? Probably!


Sleeplessness is not uncommon these days! If you have been experiencing sleepless nights or a disturbed sleep cycle, this blog might help you. In this article, we will find out what is melatonin and the importance of melatonin for sleep.


What is melatonin?


To simply put, melatonin is a hormone that is naturally produced by the pineal gland of the brain. Melatonin is responsible for regulating the circadian rhythm, which tells our body when to rest. In other words, it’s the hormone that makes us sleepy. Our brain naturally produces more melatonin as night falls. During the day, melatonin secretion is reduced as we start waking up. So, it is melatonin that helps maintain our sleep cycle and the biological sleep-wake rhythm. Did you know that melatonin production is regulated by the sun and light? As the sun sets, our brain starts to produce melatonin, which usually happens around 9 PM. Melatonin production is highest between 2 AM and 4 AM, which is why doctors and health experts advise us to hit the sack early. As discussed in our previous blog, exposure to interior lights and blue lights from phone screens can affect our melatonin secretion at night. Blue lights from phone screens suppress our melatonin production, making us feel less sleepy! If you are trying to sleep better, it is crucial to keep away from gadgets and dim the lights.

Is it safe to take melatonin long-term?

Although melatonin is safe for regular use, there are fewer published reports on its long-term usage. Also, it is probably best to avoid melatonin supplements if you are pregnant or breastfeeding or suffering from an autoimmune disease.
